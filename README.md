# My Journey in Mathematics Club (2024-2026)

## Overview:

This repo is a collection of all my learnings and work in Mathematics Club, IIT Madras, both as a Deputy Coordinator in 2024-25 and as a Coordinator in 2025-26.

## Deputy Coordinator (2024-25):

I worked in a mini-project, **`BitSync`** , in which we explored probability and information theory. I learnt about channel capacity, lossless data compression techniques, error correction codes, noisy channels, and more...

I simulated noisy channels and implemented Reed Solomon error correction codes.

* I designed questions for Mock Intern tests, Freshie Roadmap event, DC App, Advent Calendar, Bids and Brains event. [🔗](./Coordinator%20Tenure/My_Questions.pdf)

* I authored an article [**`Math Beyond the Human Mind`**](https://mathiitm.com/articles/article-on-mathematical-advancements-using-ai/), exploring advancements in Mathematics using AI. In this article, I discussed how a team of researchers in Deepmind used AI to identify hidden patterns and thus proved a conjecture in Knot Theory; how Adam Wagner used Reinforcement Learning to find Counter Examples to open conjectures in Graph Theory; how AI assistants like Lean Dojo and Lean Coilot are being used to automate proof generation and verification.

    I also discussed other ways in which AI is being used in mathematics. The article ends with a nice intriguing question:

* I spearheaded an exploratory mini-project, **`QBitSync`**, comprised of a team of 7 Deputy Coordinators in the field of Quantum Computing, exploring various quantum algorithms, including Deutsch-Jozsa, Bernstein-Vazirani, Quantum Fourier Transform, Quantum Phase Estimation, and Shor’s Algorithm. We analysed quantum speedups and documented our learnings in a structured [**`QBitSync Report`**](./Coordinator%20Tenure/QBitSync_Report.pdf). More details are in this [repository](https://github.com/Mathematics-Club-IIT-Madras/QBitSync).
